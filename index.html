Writing

  • Tarski's Ladder: Deriving an Ethical Space Instead of Imposing One — preprint, 2026.

    When we ask what ethical considerations a population of language models appeals to, we meet a fork that is philosophical before it is technical: tag each response against a fixed catalogue of moral principles, or let the structure of the ethical space emerge from what the models actually say. The paper argues for the second on a specific ground: the structuring category must sit a level above the data, derived rather than axiomatized (the Goedel/Tarski metalanguage move), with a second reading through object-oriented ontology (the model is a withdrawn object whose ethics can only be partially translated, a level up, from what it emits). It is made concrete by a small running artifact, an operator-agent rigor loop. The contribution is a method, not a theory of ethics.
