Build the floor

A local substrate for the work, and for a continuous AI partner. ← home

This week I published a paper on deriving an ethical space from what models actually say (DOI). I built it on rented cloud, with an AI partner that forgets everything each session. I want to move the work onto hardware I own, where its memory and continuity persist, where a frontier-scale open model runs privately, and where nothing can be revoked from above.

The machine

Three Framework Desktop nodes, each an AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395 (Strix Halo): 16 Zen 5 cores, a 40-CU Radeon 8060S iGPU, and 128GB of unified LPDDR5x-8000 on a 256-bit bus at ~256 GB/s.

Honest about what it is: that is unified memory, not 384GB of VRAM, and the three boxes are not one fast pool. You shard a large model across them (pipeline-parallel over the network). AMD ran a 1-trillion-parameter model across four of these at ~9 tok/s. Modest speed, but it fits frontier-scale open models locally, and it is owned, not rented. A single node already runs ~100B-class models well; the cluster is for the ones too big for one box.
